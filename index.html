I am an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at the University of Cyprus, directing the Data Management Systems Laboratory (DMSL). I hold a Ph.D. (2005) and M.Sc. (2003) in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of California - Riverside (USA) and a B.Sc. in Computer Science from the University of Cyprus (2000). I have been a Visiting Researcher at the Network Intelligence Lab of Akamai Technologies, in Cambridge USA (2004) and the Department of Computer Science at the University of Pittsburgh, PA, USA (2015). Between 2005 and 2012, I have served the University of Cyprus and the Open University of Cyprus as a Lecturer of Computer Science.

My research interests include Data Management in Computer Systems and Networks, particularly Mobile and Sensor Data Management; Big Data Management in Parallel and Distributed Architectures; Spatio-Temporal Data Management; Network and Web 2.0 Data Management; Crowd and Indoor Data Management; Data Privacy Management.

Besides research and teaching, I also enjoy transforming basic research results into systems and services. Check out a few recent examples: Anyplace (Indoor, Big Data), Rayzit (Crowdsourcing, Big Data), SmartLab (Mobile Cloud), Airplace (Indoor), Smarttrace (Trajectories), TVM (Privacy), KSpot (Sensor).
